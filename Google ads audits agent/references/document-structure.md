# Output document structure, branding and style

## Branding

- Company: **Web Marketing International Ltd**, a Google Premier Partner.
- Logo: `WMI-new-logo.png` (orange/navy). Trim whitespace and place on white.
- Palette: NAVY `#26323B` (H1/H3, table headers), ORANGE `#E8852A` (H2, accents,
  rule lines, bullets), light row shade `#F4F1EA`, exhibit panel `#F7F4ED`.
- Font: Calibri throughout. US Letter, 1 inch margins.
- Header: "Web Marketing International Ltd  |  <Doc title>". Footer: "Private &
  Confidential | Page N". Cover: logo, "Google Premier Partner", client name,
  customer ID, website, date, confidentiality line.

## Document 1 - Hybrid Audit & Growth Research

Order of sections (each H1 unless noted):

1. **Cover** (logo, title, client, customer ID, website, date).
2. **Overview** - who asked, what the doc is, the "not exhaustive" caveat, the
   quality-over-volume guiding principle.
3. **Glossary** - two-column table of the abbreviations actually used.
4. **Contents** - auto table of contents (headings 1-2).
5. **Executive Summary** - the numbers up front, then "findings that matter
   most" bullets, then a one-paragraph recommendation.
6. **PART A divider + Market, Competitor & Keyword Research**
   - The Business and Who It Sells To (+ ICP)
   - Market Research
   - Competitor Research (table: competitor / positioning / relevance & paid behaviour)
   - Keyword Research (core table + compliance/module table; volume, competition, CPC)
7. **PART B divider + Account Audit**
   - Account Analysis + Exhibit 1 (lifetime totals)
   - Campaign Structure & Settings + Exhibit 2 (top campaigns) + "what it tells us"
   - Search Terms: Where the Budget Actually Went + Exhibit 3 (real junk queries)
   - Negative Keyword Strategy
   - Auction Insights & Competitor Bidding + Exhibit 4 (domains)
   - Conversion Tracking: The Root Cause + Exhibit 5 (account-default actions) +
     "too many conversion actions" + Exhibit 6 (full action list) + OCT explainer
   - Keywords, Match Types & Ad Group Structure (phrase/exact explicit)
   - Ad Copy & Assets (full extension checklist)
   - Audiences
   - Quick Wins (bullets)
8. **PART C divider + Strategy, Forecast & Optimisation Plan**
   - Recommended Account Architecture (brand, competitor, generics, module,
     buyer, compliance, DSA, retargeting)
   - Conversion Tracking & OCT Roadmap (numbered)
   - Channel Strategy (search first, then YouTube/Demand Gen; LinkedIn/MS Ads note)
   - Short-Term Strategy (table: tactic / rationale / expected outcome)
   - Long-Term Growth Strategy (table: initiative / purpose / expected outcome)
   - Implementation Roadmap (Months 1-2 / 3-4 / 5-6)
   - Budget Recommendations (allocation table) + Management & Set-up
   - Google Ads Forecast (3-scenario table)
   - Estimated Outcomes (by phase)
   - Optimisation Plan (weeks 1-2 / 3-4 / month 2 / ongoing)
9. **Appendix: Benefits of Working with a Google Premier Partner**

## Document 2 - Website CRO Audit & Recommendations

1. Cover.

## Exhibit convention

- An "Exhibit N" is a faithful reproduction of an account or website extract as a
  styled table (navy header, alternating rows) or a panel (navy title bar +
  monospace body in a cream box), immediately followed by an italic caption:
  `Exhibit N: <what it shows>. Source: <account ID / URL>, <date>.`
- Use status colour-coding in cells: Active = green `#2E7D32`, Needs attention =
  amber `#B26A00`, Inactive = red `#C62828`, No recent conversions = grey.
- Why panels not screenshots: the browser automation cannot persist screenshots
  to disk for embedding, so reproduce the data. If the user supplies real PNGs,
  embed them in place of the matching exhibit.

## Style rules

- No em dashes. British spelling. Sentences and paragraphs, not walls of bullets.
- Address the advertiser as "you/your"; never address the commissioning colleague.
- Every diagnosis carries its evidence (a real figure or a real query).
- Confident and consultative, never salesy or hand-wavy. The Everlab and
  Sacred Science reference audits are the tone benchmark.
